Favourite Cars

Ever since I was little, I've had a fascination and admiration for 'JDM' (Japanese Domestic Market) cars.
I'm not quite sure where I first saw them (maybe in a video game), but there was something about them that caught my eye.
The first JDM car I came across and fell in love with was the Nissan GT-R (R34)

Nissan Skyline GT-R (R34)

The Nissan GT-R (R34) nicknamed 'Godzilla' is a widely known and loved JDM car. Famed for its aggresive looks and incredible performance, boasting an in-line 6-cyl, twin-turbo engine with 4WD. It is usually modified to increase its performance to mind boggling figures. Enough to give high performance 'Exotic' cars a run for their money.

Mazda RX-7

During my time learning more about JDM cars and playing video games, I got really into drifting. And when you talk about drifting and JDM cars, the car that has to be in the conversation is the RX-7. A rear wheel drive car with a roatary engine (infamously known) that can go around a corner in fashion.

Toyota Supra MK4

The Toyota Supra MK4 was a car that was able to challenge cars in a variety of races. Be it a circuit race, a sprint or a drifting challenge. Because of its rear wheel drive, you're able to have fun with it around corners and let the back slide out. But it could also output performance on par with the Nissan Skyline and outperform Exotic cars.
